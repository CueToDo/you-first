The Campaign For Representative Democracy

31st October 2020

The Rt Hon Boris Johnson, MP, The Prime Minister
House of Commons
London
SW1A 0AA

Dear Prime Minister,

We are writing to you to express our concern about

  • your government's disrespect for our laws and democracy
  • your government's mission to undermine the organisations whose duty it is to hold the government of the day to account

There are many examples which provide evidence of a "power grab" and attacks on the institutions set up to defend our laws and democratic processes.

We ask you to declare your support for a representative democracy that respects our laws, international laws, the will of the British people, their representatives, the ministerial code and the institutions which help to ensure our system of government becomes more democratic and accountable, not less.

Sincerely,

The Campaign For Representative Democracy
Other signatories

The case to answer:

1. Disrespect for our Laws: The Justice and Security Act

The Law

“ … the 2013 Justice and Security Act explicitly removed the right of the prime minister to choose the ISC [Intelligence and Security Committee] chairman and gave it to the committee members.”

Disrespect for The Law

Downing Street's attempt to impose a "preferred candidate" shows contempt for laws drafted to ensure proper democratic accountability in government.

Why this is important

The Intellligence and Security Committee Russia Report found substantial evidence that Russian interference in British politics is commonplace. We need independent bodies such as the ISC to assess the level of interference and who benefits from that interference. The fact that the government would rather you didn't know about this speaks volumes.

2. Disrespect for our Laws: The Equality Act 2010

The Law

The Equality Act 2010 legally protects people from discrimination in the workplace and in wider society.

It replaced previous anti-discrimination laws with a single Act, making the law easier to understand and strengthening protection in some situations. It sets out the different ways in which it’s unlawful to treat someone.

Disrespect for The Law

The government's hostile immigtration policy and Windrush Scandal demonstrates disrespect for laws created to protect basic human rights.

Alex Cooper · 20201125 Failure To Comply With Equality Law

BBC Radio 4, Nov 2020

On the Government's "hostile environment" and the Windrush Scandal, The Equality and Human Rights Commission concluded that "the Home Office did not comply with section 149 of the Equality Act 2010 (the Public Sector Equality Duty)."

Why this is important

Discrimination in its many forms is about an abuse of power. That could be the abuse of power one individual has over another, or the abuse of government power in denying established human rights.

The government is there to set an example to us all. When they break laws and get away with it, it's the end of a civilised society that we're all working towards.

Comment

The Home Office kept no record of those granted leave to remain and issued no paperwork - making it is difficult for Windrush arrivals to prove their legal status.

In 2010, The Home Office destroyed landing cards belonging to Windrush migrants.

"The Windrush scandal was a failure of law, policy, politics and bureaucracy."

8. Disrespect for regulations:
Failure to safeguard the public

Would Grenfell have happened if it wasn't for the government's deeply held disrespect for regulations?

The housing secretary, Robert Jenrick, “shocked” by the Grenfell revelations, insisted on the need for “stiffer regulation for building safety”, which is “too lax”. He failed to mention that the regulations are too lax because of a long history of government mania for deregulation. Or that the year before the Grenfell fire, the then business secretary, Sajid Javid, had introduced a “one in, three out” rule, meaning that for every regulation introduced, three had to be cut, and the government boasted of reducing fire safety inspections from six hours to 45 minutes.

" ... absolute power still corrupts in high places" Kenan Malik November 2020
