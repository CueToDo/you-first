The integrity of our democracy is vital and must be defended. The Electoral Commission is responsible for ensuring elections and referendums are run effectively and fairly, in accordance with the law. I am concerned that any move to restrict the Commission could undermine faith in our democracy.

Proportional Representation:
What does proportional really mean?

Proportional simply means that one thing relates directly to another.

For example, if 1 litre of petrol costs £1 then 2 litres costs £2. Price is proportional to the volume of petrol bought.

If you get paid £10 for 1 hour of work, then you should get paid £20 for 2 hours of work. Pay Is proportional to the number of hours worked.

However, in parliament the number of MPs elected for a party is NOT proportional to the number of votes cast for that party. We have a first past the post voting system. We do NOT have proportional representation.

Imagine if we introduced First Past The Post Pay. Say you worked 30 hours one week, but your colleague worked 40 hours. In a First Past The Post Pay system where the winner takes all, your colleague gets all the pay and you get nothing. Would that be fair? Of course not. Each person should be paid in proportion to the number of hours they work.

So how fair is a “winner takes all” first-past-the-post voting system?

Well, it’s not!

It’s time that the number of MPs elected for a party is proportional to the number of votes cast.

We need proportional representation now.
